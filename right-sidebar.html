Famous Origamists

There are many famous origamists such as Peter Engel, Tomoko Fuse, Makoto Yamaguchi, Satoshi Kamiya, etc. You get the point, there are many but there is one origamist consisdered the greatest origami artist in the world...

Akira Yoshizawa

For over half a century Akira Yoshizawa was the most famous origamist in the world. He introduced origami as a creative art was created many new folding techniques.

He was born in 1911 in Tochigi prefecture. His dad was a dairy farmer and he only went to school for 6 years. He moved to Tokyo when he was 13 years old and worked in a factory but continued education in evening classes. He was interested in origami and used it as a teaching support. His employers were very impressed with his skill so they let him practic origami during his work time. 1n 1937 when he was 26 he left the factory so he could devote himself to origami. Later when war began he was sent to the military hospital in Hong Kong and he would regularly decorate the beds of the paitents. In 1944 some of his creations were used in a book, "Origami Shuko" by Isao Honda.
