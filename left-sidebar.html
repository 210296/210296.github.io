Another type of Japanese paper craft is kirigami. Kirigami is the cutting of paper to make certain shapes. They can even be 3D. While origami is just folding, kirigami requires cutting and it's usually symetrical.

What is it?

Origami is the folding of paper to make different shapes and figures. It originates in Japan from ori meaning "fold" and kami (gami) meaning "paper."

More Information

The exact time origami was created is unknown and it's not created by one paticular person. Paper and paper folding was shown to Japan by the Buddhist monks in the late sixth century. Paper was considered a costly item and was used in multiple parts of Japanese life - agriculture in paticular. Certain origami figures were included in Shinto ceremonies (Shinto is the Japanese religion.)
